Introduction

I am fairly new to coding and web development. I have been working on backend and database work for roughly three months for a Utah dev shop. Much of what I know comes from the Coding Campus, a Utah based coding bootcamp specializing in Python based programming.

Prior to my current work, I was a Supply Chain Manager, and have been involved in many facets of supply chain with various manufacturing companies. I’ve always had a passion for problem solving and the closest thing to coding was creating robust spreadsheets and scenario modeling with macros and formulas on Excel. I decided to finally take the plunge and do a complete 180 degree career change in February of 2015, and dive right into coding.

The experience has been profound and I am constantly evolving in my skill set and the way I think, in terms of code, with each new thing I learn on a daily basis. The ability to instantly apply concepts and ideas upon discovery is what I love most. It’s unlike college academics, trying to retain knowledge long enough to take a test, before dumping everything out and never reflecting back on the information again.
